REVIEW

Nelson Decamps

April 21, 2026

Interstellar is emotional and powerful, mixing science and family in a way that really connects with the viewer. The story follows Cooper as he leaves his family behind to help find a new home for humanity, which creates a strong emotional conflict throughout the movie. It also reflects what can happen when the world focuses more on building weapons of mass destruction instead of taking better care of our own planet, leading to a future where Earth is no longer sustainable. At the same time, it reminds us how important human relationships are.

The movie also does a great job explaining complex ideas like space travel, black holes, and time in a way that is interesting and not too confusing. The visuals are amazing, especially the scenes in space, and they make everything feel realistic.

Overall, Interstellar is one of the best sci-fi movies because it is not just about space, but also about love, sacrifice, and the future of humanity. It makes you think deeply while also keeping you engaged the whole time.
